How Who Am I works

Each player gets a secret character — a famous person, animal, or fictional character — but they don't know who it is. By asking yes/no questions ("Am I a real person?", "Am I alive?", "Am I an animal?"), you narrow it down until you can guess. The player who figures out their character first — or with the fewest questions — wins.

Animals — the most popular category

Animals work brilliantly for Who Am I because everyone can picture them and the questions are intuitive: "Do I have four legs?", "Do I live in water?", "Am I a predator?" Even young kids can play. The difficulty comes from how well-known the animal is — everyone knows a dog, but how quickly can you guess a pangolin?

How to choose the right difficulty

The biggest mistake in Who Am I is picking characters that are too hard for your group. If someone has never heard of their character, they can't guess it no matter how good their questions are — and that's not fun for anyone.

Here's a simple rule of thumb:

Family game night with kids? Stick to easy animals and cartoons. Every character should be something a 10-year-old has heard of.

Friends in their 20s-30s? Mix easy and medium. Throw in one or two hard characters per round for the competitive players.

Team building or work event? Easy and medium only. You want everyone engaged, not one person sitting awkwardly because they got assigned an obscure film character from 1972.

Hardcore game night? Go hard. Mix categories. Turn on the timer. Watch people sweat over whether they're a Tarsier or a Kakapo.

Best questions to ask in Who Am I

Good questions eliminate as many possibilities as possible. Here are the most effective opening questions by category:

Universal openers (work for any category)

"Am I a real person?" eliminates all fictional characters and animals in one shot. "Am I alive today?" splits real people in half. "Am I human?" separates animals from everything else. Three questions, and you've narrowed 900 characters down to about 100.

For animals

"Do I have four legs?", "Do I live in water?", "Am I a predator?", "Do I live in Africa?", "Can I fly?", "Am I bigger than a dog?" — these are built into the game's hint system and they're effective because each one splits the remaining animals roughly in half.

For film characters

"Am I from a movie made after 2000?", "Am I a villain?", "Am I a superhero?", "Am I animated?" — these structural questions narrow the field quickly without relying on specific film knowledge.

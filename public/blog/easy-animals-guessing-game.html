Best questions to ask in animal guessing games

The key to guessing animals efficiently is asking questions that cut the remaining options in half. Here are the 10 most useful opening questions, roughly in order:

🦴

Is it a mammal? — Eliminates birds, fish, reptiles, insects in one go.

🌊

Does it live in water? — Separates ocean creatures from everything else.

🐾

Does it have 4 legs? — Splits mammals from birds, snakes, fish.

🌍

Does it live in Africa? — Narrows down the continent quickly.

🦅

Can it fly? — Identifies birds and a few surprises (bats, flying squirrels).

📏

Is it bigger than a dog? — Great for separating large from small animals.

🏡

Could you keep it as a pet? — Quickly identifies domestic vs wild animals.

🥩

Is it a predator? — Separates hunters from prey and herbivores.

🌙

Is it nocturnal? — Surprisingly useful for narrowing down mid-game.

⚠️

Is it endangered? — Eliminates common farm and pet animals immediately.

💡 Strategy tip: Never guess the specific animal until you've narrowed it down to 2-3 options. Each wrong guess is a wasted question. Use broad questions first, then get specific.

Picking animals for different groups

Kids under 8: Stick to farm animals and the most famous wild ones — dog, cat, cow, elephant, lion, penguin, fish, frog. Keep to 10-15 options so they have a realistic chance of guessing.

Mixed ages (family game night): Use the full easy list. Kids will guess dog and cat quickly, and grown-ups can help with flamingo and chameleon. Everyone stays engaged.

Adults only: Mix medium and hard animals. Platypus and Pangolin are funny because people know what they are but can't figure out the right questions. Throw in an Okapi or Shoebill for the wildlife nerds.

Team building / work events: Easy animals only, but use the full list of 71. The point is fun, not frustration. Nobody wants to be the person who can't guess "dog" in front of their colleagues.

Frequently asked questions

What are good animals for guessing games?

The best animals for guessing games are ones everyone can picture: dog, cat, elephant, lion, penguin, dolphin. They have iconic features (trunk, stripes, fins) that make yes/no questions productive.

What animals are good for kids in Who Am I?

For kids, pick animals they know from everyday life or cartoons: dog, cat, horse, chicken, fish, frog, duck, rabbit, cow, pig. These have clear, simple features that children can ask about.

How do you guess animals in 20 questions?

Start broad: Is it a mammal? Does it live in water? Is it bigger than a dog? Does it live on a farm? Each answer eliminates half the remaining options. Save specific guesses for when you've narrowed it down to 2-3 possibilities.

What is the hardest animal to guess?

Animals most people haven't heard of — like Okapi, Shoebill, Fossa, Aye-aye, or Kakapo — are nearly impossible. Even well-known but unusual animals like Platypus, Pangolin, or Narwhal can be tricky because they defy expectations.

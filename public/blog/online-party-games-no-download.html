Six free party games that work right now

Forbidden Words — the classic description game

Teams take turns describing words without using the forbidden expressions. A referee from the opposing team watches for slip-ups. Fast, loud, endlessly replayable. Works for 4-12 people and takes about 20 minutes for a full game. Great for parties, family gatherings, and work events.

2 Truths 1 Lie — the one that reveals everything

Write two true things and one lie about yourself. Everyone else votes on which is the lie. Sounds simple until someone claims they once had dinner with a prime minister and it turns out to be true. Works for 3-20 people and is genuinely the best ice breaker ever invented.

Sketch & Guess — no artistic skill required

Words travel through a chain of drawings and guesses. What starts as "Eiffel Tower" ends up as "wizard's hat" after four rounds. No points, no winning — just a reveal screen that produces the best screenshots you'll see all year. Best for 3-10 people.

Who Am I? — for in-person or video calls

A character is assigned to you — a famous person, animal, or fictional character. You can't see who you are. Ask yes/no questions to figure it out before time runs out. Works in voice mode (for in-person play) or chat mode (for online groups). 3-15 players.

Corporate Bingo — the secret work game

A bingo card full of office buzzwords. Mark them off during a meeting without anyone knowing you're playing. First to get a full line wins. Also works as a pure party game — read out phrases and see who fills their card first. Up to 50 players.

Dots & Boxes — the thinking game

Take turns drawing lines on a grid. Close a box, claim it, play again. The tension when someone is about to hand you a chain of ten boxes is genuinely nail-biting. Best for 2-4 players who want something quieter and more strategic.

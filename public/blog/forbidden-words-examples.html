Need word ideas for Forbidden Words? Here are 100 tried-and-tested examples — each with 5 forbidden clues — sorted by difficulty. Use them at your next party, team event, or game night with friends.

How Forbidden Words works

One player sees a word and 5 forbidden clue words. They have to describe the secret word to their team without using any of the forbidden words. The opposing team's referee watches for slip-ups. If you accidentally say a forbidden word — buzz! The team loses that word and moves to the next one.

The challenge is that the forbidden words are exactly the ones you'd instinctively reach for. Describing "pizza" without saying "cheese", "dough" or "Italian"? That's where the fun begins.

Tips for picking great words

The best words for Forbidden Words share a few things in common. Everyone should know the word — obscure vocabulary isn't fun, it's frustrating. The forbidden clues should block the obvious descriptions, but leave creative side-routes open. A word like "pizza" is perfect because you can't say "cheese" or "Italian", but you could talk about delivery, slices, or midnight snacks.

Mix difficulties throughout the game rather than doing all easy words first. This keeps everyone engaged — beginners get their moments, and experienced players get challenged.

For team events and work settings, consider adding industry-specific words that your group would know. A marketing team describing "SEO" without saying "Google", "search", "ranking", "website" or "traffic"? That's a memorable round.

💡 Pro tip: The hardest words aren't the obscure ones — they're the simple concepts where the forbidden words block every obvious route. Try describing "water" without saying "drink", "ocean", "river", "wet" or "liquid". Suddenly the simplest word becomes the hardest challenge.

Frequently asked questions

What are forbidden words in the game?

In Forbidden Words, one player describes a secret word to their team — but they cannot use the 5 forbidden clue words listed on the card. For example, if the word is PIZZA, you might not be allowed to say "cheese", "Italian", "dough", "oven" or "toppings". Your team has to guess the word from your creative description.

How many words do you need for a game of Forbidden Words?

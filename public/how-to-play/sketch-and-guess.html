How to Play Sketch & Guess Online?

Sketch & Guess is a no-points party game where words pass through a chain of drawings and guesses — and get hilariously distorted along the way. Free, for 4-10 players, playable on any device.

The Chain Mechanic

Each player starts their own chain. Words rotate around the group — alternating between drawing and guessing — until every player has contributed to every chain.

✏️Player 1 writes: "Elephant"
🎨Player 2 draws their best elephant
🤔Player 3 guesses: "Grey blob with a hose?"
🎨Player 4 draws a grey blob with a hose
🎉Everyone sees the full chain — and laughs

Step by Step

  • Step 1 — Write a word: Every player secretly writes a word or short phrase (anything — animals, objects, concepts, famous people)
  • Step 2 — Draw it: You receive someone else's word and draw it on the canvas. You have 80 seconds
  • Step 3 — Guess it: You receive a drawing and write what you think it shows. You have 30 seconds
  • Repeat: Drawing and guessing alternate until the chain is complete
  • Reveal: Everyone sees the full evolution of their original word

No Points — Pure Fun

Sketch & Guess has no scoring system. There is no winner. The reward is watching your "Eiffel Tower" turn into a "pointy hat" and then a "wizard" over the course of 4 rounds. The laughs at the reveal screen are the whole point.

💡 Tip: You don't need to be a good artist — bad drawings make the game funnier. The worse the drawing, the more unexpected the guess, and the better the reveal.

Timers

  • Writing a word: 30 seconds
  • Drawing: 80 seconds (configurable by host)
  • Guessing: 30 seconds (configurable by host)

If time runs out before you submit, whatever you have typed or drawn is submitted automatically so the game keeps moving.

How Many Players?

Sketch & Guess works best with 5-8 players. With 4 players the chains are short but still fun. With 8-10 players the chains are longer and more chaotic — which usually produces funnier results.

Play Again

After the reveal the host can start a new game with the same group. Everyone stays in the room — no need to reshare the link.

💡 Best words to write: Specific things work better than abstract concepts — "dachshund", "spaghetti", "Mount Everest" will produce funnier chains than "happiness" or "love".
